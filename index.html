Spitzer Rules

Spitzer is a four player trick-taking card game where partnerships vary from hand to hand. Players on the team that takes the most card points each hand earn game points. The first player to reach 42 game points wins.

Deck: Play with all cards 7 and up, including Aces (the 7, 8, 9, K, 10, A, J, and Q of every suit)

Trump: The order of trump, from the highest to lowest, is: Q♣, 7♦, Q♠, Q♥, Q♦, J♣, J♠, J♥, J♦, A♦, 10♦, K♦, 9♦, 8♦

Note: The terms “Trump” and “Diamonds” always refer to the set of all trump cards, which includes all the Jacks, Queens, and the remaining diamond cards. “Offsuit” refers to an off suit (♣, ♠, or ♥) set of cards. The order of offsuit cards, from highest to lowest, is: A, 10, K, 9, 8, 7.

Play: Someone deals. 8 cards are dealt. They can be dealt 3 to each player, then 2, then 3.

Teams: In a normal hand, teams are the players with the two black queens (the “Black Queens Team”) and the other two players (the “Non-Black-Queens Team”).

Calling a Partner or Zola: Players may choose to play alone without a partner in order to earn more game points - but failure to take the needed amount of card points during the hand will result in the other players earning extra points. To see how many game points are earned, experiment with applying these calls in the Score tab.

  • Calling for a partner: If a player has both Black Queens, he may call for a partner by naming an Ace of an offsuit. The calling player must have an offsuit card of matching suit. The player with that Ace will join the calling player on the Black Queens team. If no Ace is available to call (for example, if the calling player has each offsuit Ace) then the calling player may announce this fact and choose to be partners with the player who takes the first trick. Team must take over 60 card points to win game points as normal.
  • Going Sneaker: If a player has both Black Queens, he may “go Sneaker” by not calling a partner, therefore going alone as the only player on the Black Queens team. Player must take over 60 card points to win extra game points.
  • Calling Zola: A player may choose to call Zola, meaning he is declaring to go alone and must take over 60 card points to win many extra game points.
  • Calling Zola Schneider: A player may choose to call Zola Schneider, meaning he is declaring to go alone and must take over 90 card points to win a huge amount of extra game points.
  • Calling Zola Schneider Schwartz: A player may choose to call Zola Schneider Schwartz, meaning he is declaring to go alone with the aim of taking all the tricks. The player must take all the tricks in order to instantly win the game. Failure to do so will result in the other players winning the game instead.

Trick Playing and Taking: The first trick is led by the player to the left of the dealer, and, continuing clockwise, each player plays a card. The player with the highest card takes the trick and leads the next trick. The following rules must be followed when playing a card, listed in order of priority (see the Play Chart for an interactive flowchart):

  1. Follow suit - if you can follow suit, you must
  2. Play trump - if you cannot follow suit and you have a trump card, you must play trump
  3. Beat the current high card - if you can beat the current high card in the trick, you must

After all tricks in a hand have been played, game points are applied and the next player deals the next hand.

Scoring Game Points: In a normal hand (where no player has gone alone by going sneaker or calling a Zola) the Black Queens Team must take over 60 card points to earn game points and 90 or more points to earn extra game points. If the Black Queens team fails, the Non-Black-Queens team earns game points based on how many card points were taken.

When the Non-Black-Queens team takes 31 or more card points, they have prevented the Black Queens team from taking 90 or more card points and it is said that they have earned “Brush”. If the Non-Black-Queens team takes over 60 card points, they have set the Black Queens team and earn game points.

The first player to 42 game points wins.

App Instructions

  • To start scoring a game, enter player names and tap Play
  • Tap a player’s name to add them to the Black Queens team, and tap them again to undo that
  • Tap the “+” on a player to add a call for them
  • Then select how many point cards the calling/Black Queen team won during the hand
  • Double tap Apply Points to add the points and get ready for the next hand.
